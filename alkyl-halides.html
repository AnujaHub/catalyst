Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers

Oxidation of Alcohols

R–OH + [O] →

Answer: Alcohols can be oxidized to aldehydes, ketones, or carboxylic acids depending on the reaction conditions (e.g., K₂Cr₂O₇ for strong oxidation).

Reimer-Tiemann Reaction

Phenol + CHCl₃ + NaOH

Answer: This reaction introduces a formyl group (-CHO) at the ortho or para position to the hydroxyl group in phenol.

Williamson Ether Synthesis

R–Br + NaOEt → R–O–R'

Answer: Ethers are synthesized by the reaction of an alkyl halide with a strong alkoxide ion (NaOEt) in an SN2 reaction.
