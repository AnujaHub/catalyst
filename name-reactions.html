Name Reactions

Wurtz Reaction

Reaction: 2R–X + 2Na → R–R + 2NaX

Answer: The Wurtz reaction involves the coupling of two alkyl halides (R-X) with sodium metal to form a new alkane (R-R).

Friedel–Crafts Alkylation

Reaction: C₆H₆ + R–Cl → C₆H₅–R (in presence of AlCl₃)

Answer: The Friedel–Crafts alkylation adds an alkyl group (R) to an aromatic ring (C₆H₆) using an alkyl halide and a Lewis acid (AlCl₃) as a catalyst.

Friedel–Crafts Acylation

Reaction: C₆H₆ + RCOCl → C₆H₅–CO–R (in presence of AlCl₃)

Answer: The Friedel–Crafts acylation is the addition of an acyl group (RCO) to an aromatic ring (C₆H₆) using an acyl halide and a Lewis acid (AlCl₃) as a catalyst.

Aldol Condensation

Reaction: CH₃CHO + CH₃CHO → CH₂=CH–CO–CH₃ + H₂O (in presence of base)

Answer: The Aldol condensation involves the reaction of an aldehyde (or ketone) with a base to form β-hydroxy ketones, which undergo dehydration to yield α,β-unsaturated carbonyl compounds.

Kolbe Schmitt Reaction

Reaction: C₆H₆ + CO₂ → C₆H₄OHCOOH (in presence of NaOH)

Answer: The Kolbe-Schmitt reaction involves the reaction of phenol with carbon dioxide under high pressure and temperature to form salicylic acid.

Cannizzaro Reaction

Reaction: 2RCHO → RCH₂OH + RCOOH (in presence of NaOH)

Answer: The Cannizzaro reaction is the disproportionation of non-enolizable aldehydes in the presence of a base, forming an alcohol and a carboxylate anion.

Bayer Villiger Oxidation

Reaction: R₂C=O + RCO₃H → R₂C(OH)R + CO₂

Answer: The Bayer-Villiger oxidation involves the oxidation of ketones to esters by reacting them with a peracid.

Grignard Reaction

Reaction: RMgX + R'COOH → R–R' + MgX₂

Answer: The Grignard reaction involves the addition of Grignard reagents (RMgX) to carbonyl compounds, producing alcohols.

Hoffmann Bromamide Degradation

Reaction: RCONH₂ + Br₂ → RNH₂ + CO₂ (in presence of NaOH)

Answer: The Hoffmann degradation involves the conversion of an amide into an amine with the loss of a carbon atom under the influence of bromine and a strong base.

Williamson Ether Synthesis

Reaction: R–X + RO– → R–O–R + X–

Answer: The Williamson ether synthesis involves the reaction of an alkoxide ion with a primary alkyl halide to form an ether.

Rosenmund Reduction

Reaction: RCOCl + H₂ → RCHO (in presence of Pd/BaSO₄)

Answer: The Rosenmund reduction involves the reduction of acyl chlorides to aldehydes using hydrogen gas in the presence of palladium on barium sulfate.

Fischer Esterification

Reaction: RCOOH + R'OH → RCOOR' + H₂O (in presence of acid)

Answer: The Fischer esterification is the formation of an ester by reacting a carboxylic acid with an alcohol in the presence of an acid catalyst.

Diels-Alder Reaction

Reaction: C₄H₆ + C₂H₄ → C₆H₁₀

Answer: The Diels-Alder reaction involves the cycloaddition between a diene and a dienophile to form a six-membered ring.

Wittig Reaction

Reaction: R₃P⁺–C(−) + R'CHO → R₂C=CR' + R₃P=O

Answer: The Wittig reaction involves the reaction of a phosphonium ylide with a carbonyl compound to form an alkene.

Mannich Reaction

Reaction: CH₂O + NH₃ + CH₃CO– → β-amino ketone

Answer: The Mannich reaction is a condensation of an amine, formaldehyde, and a compound containing an active hydrogen atom (like a ketone or aldehyde) to form a β-amino carbonyl compound.
